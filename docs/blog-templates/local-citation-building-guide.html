Premium SEO Content

Local citations are online mentions of your business NAP — Name, Address, and Phone number. They are a foundational local ranking factor. Google uses citation consistency and volume to determine whether your business is legitimate and where it's located. Inconsistent citations are one of the top reasons local businesses fail to rank.

Citation Types: Structured vs. Unstructured

Structured citations appear on business directories and data aggregators like Yelp, Yellow Pages, and Bing Places. They have dedicated NAP fields and are submitted through a formal listing process. Unstructured citations appear in blog posts, news articles, social media mentions, and event pages where your business is referenced in natural content. Both types matter, but structured citations are easier to control and audit.

Top Citation Sites

  • Core: Google Business Profile, Yelp, Bing Places, Apple Maps, Facebook, Yellow Pages
  • Data Aggregators: Infogroup, Neustar Localeze, Factual, Foursquare
  • Industry-Specific: Healthgrades (healthcare), Avvo (legal), Houzz (home services), TripAdvisor (hospitality)
  • Local: Chamber of Commerce, Better Business Bureau, local business journals

NAP Consistency Is Critical

Your Name, Address, and Phone number must be identical across every citation. Even small variations — "St." vs. "Street," "Ste." vs. "Suite," different area codes — confuse Google and weaken your local authority. Use a citation audit tool to scan for inconsistencies and duplicates.

Citation Audit Process

Run a citation audit quarterly. Export all existing citations into a spreadsheet. Flag any NAP inconsistencies, duplicate listings, or outdated information. Submit correction requests and monitor changes. Tools like BrightLocal, Whitespark, and Moz Local automate citation tracking and correction. A clean citation profile directly correlates with higher Local Pack rankings.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many citations do I need?
Quality over quantity. 15-25 high-quality, consistent citations on authoritative platforms are more valuable than 100 low-quality directory listings.
Do citations still matter for local SEO?
Absolutely. Citations are a top local ranking factor. Google's local algorithm relies heavily on citation signals to verify business legitimacy and location accuracy.
