Blogger vs WordPress is one of the longest-running debates in the blogging world. Both platforms can rank well, but they take different paths to SEO success. Blogger vs WordPress SEO compares their strengths, limitations, and ideal use cases for search visibility.

SEO Capabilities Comparison

WordPress SEO Advantages

WordPress offers unmatched SEO flexibility through plugins like Yoast SEO, Rank Math, and All in One SEO. You get full control over meta tags, schema markup, XML sitemaps, canonical URLs, redirects, and robots.txt. The plugin ecosystem makes advanced SEO accessible to beginners.

Blogger SEO Advantages

Blogger is built on Google's infrastructure, giving it native speed and reliability advantages. Google-hosted sites get preferential crawling and indexing. Blogger automatically generates sitemaps, handles mobile responsiveness, and offers built-in AMP support without plugins.

Speed & Performance

Blogger wins on default speed because it's hosted on Google's servers with built-in CDN. WordPress speed depends entirely on your hosting provider, theme quality, and optimization plugins. A well-optimized WordPress site on quality hosting can match Blogger, but Blogger is faster out of the box.

Control & Customization

WordPress wins on control. You can modify every aspect of SEO: .htaccess, server-level caching, database optimization, custom post types, and advanced schema markup. Blogger is limited to XML theme editing and built-in settings. For SEO specialists, WordPress offers more levers to pull.

Key Insight: Choose Blogger if you want a free, fast, maintenance-free platform with solid built-in SEO. Choose WordPress if you need advanced SEO control, scalability for large sites, or plan to monetize heavily with complex sales funnels.

Cost & Scalability

Blogger is free with unlimited bandwidth on Google's infrastructure. WordPress costs vary: hosting ($5-100/month), premium themes ($30-100), plugins ($0-200/year), and maintenance. For high-traffic sites, WordPress scales better with more optimization options, but at a higher cost.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which platform ranks higher on Google?
Both can rank equally well. Google does not favor its own platform. Rankings depend on content quality, optimization, and backlinks, not platform choice.
Can I migrate from Blogger to WordPress?
Yes. WordPress has built-in Blogger import tools. Set up proper 301 redirects to preserve SEO value during migration.
Is Blogger dying?
No. Blogger is still actively maintained by Google and remains a solid platform, especially for hobby bloggers and niche sites.
