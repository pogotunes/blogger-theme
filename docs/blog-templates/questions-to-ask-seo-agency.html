Interviewing an SEO agency requires asking the right questions to determine if they're truly capable of delivering results. These 15 essential questions cover strategy, reporting, communication, and ethics — so you can hire with confidence.

Strategy & Approach

  1. "Walk me through your SEO process from start to finish." — A clear process shows maturity. Vague answers suggest they lack a systematic approach.
  2. "How do you approach keyword research?" — Look for depth beyond search volume: search intent, difficulty, SERP features, and opportunity analysis.
  3. "What does your content strategy look like?" — They should discuss topic clusters, pillar pages, content gaps, and aligning content to the buyer journey.
  4. "How do you handle technical SEO?" — Ask about their audit tools, process for Core Web Vitals, schema implementation, and site architecture improvements.
  5. "What's your link building methodology?" — White-hat only. Watch for mentions of PBNs, link farms, or automated outreach as red flags.

Reporting & Measurement

  1. "What metrics do you report on monthly?" — They should track organic traffic, keyword rankings, conversion rate, backlink growth, and ROI — not just impressions.
  2. "How do you attribute results to your work?" — Especially important if other marketing channels are active. They should explain their tracking and attribution methodology.
  3. "Can you show me a sample report?" — A real report reveals transparency, detail level, and their ability to communicate insights clearly.
  4. "How do you handle ranking fluctuations?" — They should explain normal volatility, algorithm updates, and how they differentiate between temporary drops and real issues.

Communication & Account Management

  1. "Who will be my main point of contact?" — You want a dedicated account manager, not a rotating team or junior staff without oversight.
  2. "How often will we communicate?" — Best practices: weekly email updates, bi-weekly calls, and monthly strategy reviews.
  3. "What happens if my account manager leaves?" — Ask about transition processes and team redundancy to ensure continuity.

Ethics & Expectations

  1. "What's your stance on black-hat SEO?" — Any hedging or "it depends" answers are dangerous. They should unequivocally follow Google's guidelines.
  2. "Can you provide references from clients in similar industries?" — Ask to speak directly with 2-3 current or past clients about their experience.
  3. "What results are realistic for my business in the first 6 months?" — Honest agencies set realistic expectations. Promises of #1 rankings in 30 days are a clear red flag.

Bonus Questions

Ask about their experience with your specific CMS (WordPress, Shopify, Blogger), their approach to AI search optimization (Google SGE, ChatGPT), and how they handle algorithm updates like core updates and helpful content updates.
