Premium SEO Content

Ecommerce SEO is fundamentally different from traditional SEO. With hundreds or thousands of product pages, faceted navigation, duplicate content risks, and intense competition, online stores need a specialized approach. This ecommerce SEO checklist covers the 30 most impactful actions for ranking your online store.

Product Page Optimization

Every product page needs a unique title tag (50-60 chars) with primary keyword at the start, a compelling meta description (150-160 chars) with a value proposition, and a unique product description that's not copied from the manufacturer. Optimize product images with descriptive filenames and alt text. Include customer reviews — they add fresh unique content naturally.

Category SEO

Category pages often rank for high-volume terms. Use descriptive, keyword-rich category names. Write 100-200 words of unique content above or below the product grid. Add subcategory pages for a logical hierarchy. Include breadcrumb navigation for clear site architecture signals. Avoid thin content category pages — they get filtered out of search results.

Faceted Navigation & Thin Content

Faceted navigation filters (size, color, price) create thousands of parameter-based URLs that can cause duplicate content issues. Implement noindex tags on low-value filter combinations. Use rel=canonical to consolidate similar pages. Block unimportant parameter URLs in robots.txt or Google Search Console. Filter combinations that don't add value should be excluded from indexing entirely.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does ecommerce SEO take to work?
Product pages can rank in 2-4 weeks for low-competition terms. Competitive category pages typically take 3-6 months of consistent optimization effort.
Is duplicate content a problem for ecommerce sites?
It's the biggest ecommerce SEO issue. Manufacturer descriptions, similar product variants, and faceted navigation all create duplicate content. Every product needs truly unique content.
