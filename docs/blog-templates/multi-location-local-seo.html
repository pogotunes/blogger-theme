Managing local SEO for multiple locations presents unique challenges. Each location needs its own identity, citations, content, and link profile while maintaining brand consistency. Without a scalable strategy, multi-location businesses dilute their visibility and miss local customers at every branch.

Multi-Location GBP Management

Create a location group in Google Business Profile Manager. Each location gets its own verified profile with unique categories, descriptions, photos, and posts. Never use the same phone number or address for different locations. Use the GBP bulk management features to update information across all locations simultaneously while preserving per-location customization.

Location Pages That Work

Build a dedicated location page for each branch on your website. Each page must include: unique 300+ word content, NAP, Google Map embed, local testimonials, unique photos of that specific location, and local schema markup. Avoid duplicate content across location pages — write unique content about the specific neighborhood, team, and services each location offers.

Local Link Building Per Location

Each location needs its own local backlinks. Sponsor local events in each community, join the local chamber of commerce, partner with local charities, and get mentioned in local news outlets. Links should point to the specific location page, not the homepage. A local link from a city newspaper or community organization carries significant local relevance signals.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a separate GBP for each location?
Yes. Each physical location needs its own verified Google Business Profile with unique contact info, categories, and content. This is non-negotiable.
How do I avoid duplicate content on location pages?
Write unique content for each location. Highlight local landmarks, neighborhood details, local team members, and location-specific services. Use different testimonials and photos for each page.
